# Unemployment Trends in Canada: A Provincial and Demographic Analysis  
### *Data Analytics & Visualization*  
**Capstone Project 1**  
Understanding the Canadian unemployment rate and its emerging trends.

---

### Project Overview

The unemployment rate is a key indicator of a country’s labor market health. In Canada, unemployment rates vary across provinces and demographic groups due to differences in economic structure, population, and economic recessions. Major events, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, have significantly impacted job security for many Canadians.  

This project analyzes historical labor market data from 1976 to the present to uncover long-term trends, regional disparities, and demographic patterns, providing insights that can inform policy-making and workforce planning.

---

### Objectives

This analysis aims to answer the following questions:  

- How have national and provincial unemployment rates evolved over time?  
- What patterns exist in unemployment rates across provinces?  
- Which demographic groups are most affected by unemployment trends?  
- How can data visualizations communicate key insights effectively?
